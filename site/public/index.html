About Me

Trowbridge born and bred, I moved out to Plymouth to complete a BSc (Hons) in Geography. During this time; I played, and was on the committee, for the university hockey team, took a year in industry where I worked as an Education Assistant at Field Student Council Preston Montford and completed at dissertation on the Vulnerability of Wooded Landscapes to Disease in England and Wales. After graduating with a 2:1, I moved to Bristol (after a short while back at home) and started working for Simitive, a small IT company, as a project manager. I have now successfully implemented the Simitive product with a number of clients within the UK and further abroad.

Being passionate about the environment, I volunteered for Wiltshire Wildlife Trust in the short time I was home after university and I am currently volunteering for Avon Wildlife Trust. These roles involve undertaking practical conservation, volunteer management and leading family events. Often I can be found outdoors or trying to persuade my friends and family to go outdoors with me. If this is not the case, I may be working on a self-designed sewing project or learning to like (and hopefully get good at) cooking.

Right now I am hoping to improve my flora and fauna identification skills, learn more about the habitats around me and our impact on them. I would ideally like a role where I make a difference by connecting people with nature and protecting the environment for the future. My perfect role would encourage continued professional and personal development and present further career opportunities.
